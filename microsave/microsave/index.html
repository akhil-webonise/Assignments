India is a social welfare state. Rs. 2,700 billion (US$43 billion) have been allocated for subsidies in FY 2015-16. The enormous scale and the sheer number of people involved in the logistics of ferrying food grains, cooking gas and a host of other commodities across the country leads to "leakages".To achieve the dual objectives of: i) limiting subsidy outlay through de-duplication; and ii) achieving efficiency in payment transfers.     The actual performance or utilization of accessed funds vary from the initially intended purposes in most cases in poverty sector since social values system contextually both at individual level, house hold level and societal level act strong drivers of human behavior diluting or diverting the well nurtured financial capability from going towards indented goal. From ethical perspectives it is unfair and misutilisation in banking parlance but social values both at individual and household level force to perform for the immediate needs morally as the breadwinner of HH .
